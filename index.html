About the Show

Doctor Who is both a television show and a global media franchise owned and operated by the British Broadcasting Company, BBC. It centers on a time traveling man known as The Doctor and his adventures in saving the world. The fast paced narrative allows for the Doctor's regeneration, which has lead to multiple actors taking over the lead role throughout it's long run as an entertainment staple world-wide. The Doctor is often accompanied by one of his many companions, with the revived show typically only having one companion per season, or even per doctor. The show's timeline can be confusing, with all the wibbly wobbly timey wimey stuff, but the show's captivating characters and narrative have brought in audiences for generations.

    2010-2013

Who is the Doctor?

"The Doctor" is the primary alias of a renegade Time Lord from Gallifrey, who travels through space in time with various companions, in a stolen TARDIS. He has been referred to as the Universe's "greatest defender" due to his heroic efforts savign the cosmos from hundreds of would-be disasters. He largely belives in non-violent conflict resolution, using his wit and creativity to overcome potentially fatal situations, but is willing to assume the position of warrior if the situation calls for it. Many find themselves confused by the number of faces that "The Doctor" had, but the Time Lord race is equipped with the power of regneration, allowing them to use energy to resume life with primarily surface changes. By in large, The Doctor is known for his heroism and charm, able to save the universe for over 1000 years and entertain audiences for over 50 years.

The TARDIS

TARDIS stands for Time And Relative Dimension in Space. It is the Doctor's spaceship, allowing him to traverse not only through space but time as well. He stole it out of desparation. Shaped like an old London Police Box, it is bigger on the inside. The TARDIS changes slightly from Doctor to Doctor. But it is more than a ship, it is more like a steady companion to the Doctor.

What is a "Whovian"?

A "whovian" is a member of the Doctor Who fandom. The welcoming community of science fiction fans span a wide variety of ages, cultures and communities. The term originated in the 1980s among American fans with the Doctor Who Fan Club of America and their publication of the Whovian Times.

The second incarnation of the show along with the influence of the internet and events like Comic Con sparked a whole new level of what it means to be a Whovian. Now fans follow blogs on Tumbr, write their own fan fiction, cosplay at major show events, but most importantly love and appreciate the show with every chance they have to interact with it.
